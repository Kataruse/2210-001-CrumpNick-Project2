Sorting Algorithms Report

By: Nick Crump & Nick Trahan

Introduction

In this project, we used a Iterative (Cocktail Sort) and Recursive (QuickSort) Sorting Algorthim to sort multiple Int and Book data sets to test the speeds of the algortihms. Within these tests, we found the following data.

Cocktail Sort

Cocktail Sort is an Iterative Sorting Algorithm known by many names, such as the Shaker Sort, Shuttle Sort, Happy Hour Sort, and Ripple Sort. The complexity at its best case is O(n), and at it's worse its O(n2) which is also its average case. It is a variation of Bubble Sort

QuickSort

QuickSort is a Recursive Sorting Algorithm based on Divide-and-Conquer. The complexity at its best case is Ω ( n log (n)), and at it's worse its O(n2) while at θ ( n log (n)) for it's average case.

Predictions

We both predict that QuickSort will be faster, and by quite a bit too.
